# User Study Report

---

## User 1

### 1. User Information
- Relationship to user:  Friend
- Prior experience with app: None
- Experience with similar apps: Some

---

### 2. Observation Context
- Location of study: Home room
- Device used: laptop
- Instructions / tasks given: (1) Look at certain types of memes. (2) Rate 5 memes. (3) Upload their own meme and see the results.

---

### 3. Three Things the User Liked (Functionality Only)
-  Liked that the buttons to change types meme categories on the home page were easy to see.
-  Liked that he can easily sign in and sign out.
-  Liked the voting mechanics (right side green for yes, and left side red for no)

---

### 4. Three Areas for Improvement
-  Wants the meme to expand into large size when clicked.
-  Voting button at the top not the most clear, hard to find.
-  Insteading of showing all captions at once, show one at a time and allow used to click through each of them.

---

### 5. Observed Friction or Confusion
-  Confused why memes couldn't be clicked on to show more info or exapnd.
-  Confused because user thought he was supposed to select a caption out of all the the generate ones.

---

### 6. Behavioral Observations
- On the home page, user noticed the different meme categories right away.   
- User waited for instructions as opposed to exploring on their own.
- User was hesitant about how to find the voting section of the website.
- User was confident in figuring out how to upload an image to create their own meme and understand the results of that page.

## Final Summary
### Things you learned from observing users
I learned that the user looks at common interactions first, like the buttons at the top, so havng things that users can click on is best. Having straightforward flows also is good, such as following the steps one at a time to upload an image and then getting the captions.

### Things you found surprising
I was suprised that a user was a bit confused with the voting system and didn't understand it at first. I was also surprised that users wanted more interactions.

### Patterns you noticed across multiple users
Some paterns that I noticed was that the users expected the memes to be clickable as opposed to just displayed as they are. I also noticed that the users liked having the memes be put into groupings and they can view each type of meme separately.

### At least three specific improvements you plan to make to your application based on this study
(1) I will make the memes on the home page expand when they are clicked on
(2) I will create a loading circle to indicate that the captions were still being generated and for the user to wait
(3) I will improve the navigation at the top, making it so that getting to the voting page and the upload/generate caption page easier to see and get to.
